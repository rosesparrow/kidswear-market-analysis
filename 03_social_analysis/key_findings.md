# Key Findings – Social Media Analysis (March 2026)

Data snapshot: March 2026 | 21 brands across budget, mass, mid-market, lifestyle, UK premium, US premium

=======================================================================
KEY FINDINGS — Social Media Analysis
=======================================================================

1. SCALE LANDSCAPE: Mass market dominates follower volume.
 M&S, Next, ASOS, H&M, Zara, Primark hold the largest audiences.
 Independent/lifestyle brands (Lounge, Brandy Melville) demonstrate
 meaningful scale can be built on brand identity alone at mid-market prices.

2. ENGAGEMENT INVERSE: Smaller/identity-led brands punch above their weight.
 Pink Palm Puff         676K IG followers    eng rate:  2.366%
 Roller Rabbit          258K IG followers    eng rate:  1.157%
 Desmond & Dempsey      152K IG followers    eng rate:  0.197%
 Babble & Goose          64K IG followers    eng rate:  0.182%
 [New brands — see notebook for full table]
 Mass-market brands (M&S, Next, H&M, Zara) fall below 0.1–0.2%.

3. CONTENT FORMAT: Static posts essentially dead across all brands.
 Carousel dominant for lifestyle/premium brands (storytelling, depth).
 Reels used by mass brands for algorithmic reach/discovery.

4. TIKTOK DIVIDE:
 Budget/mass UK brands have TikTok presence but low engagement velocity.
 Mid-market aesthetic brands (Zara, River Island) have accounts but
 limited native strategy. Only PPP and White Fox show meaningful TikTok scale.
 Matalan: TikTok only (no Instagram account).

5. PINK PALM PUFF — BENCHMARK CASE STUDY:
 TikTok: ~967K followers | IG: ~676K followers
 Engagement rate: 2.366% — highest in dataset
 Follower/following ratio: ~12,291:1 — signals strong organic earned growth
 All-carousel IG content. TikTok-native founder-as-influencer model.
 IMPLICATION: Demonstrates that strong brand identity + TikTok-native
 content strategy can build a highly engaged teen apparel audience.
 The model is platform-first, not price-point dependent.
